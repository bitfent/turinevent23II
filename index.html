FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is a digital asset infrastructure provider?

A digital asset infrastructure provider offers core systems for managing digital assets — including accounts, payments, custody, conversion, and settlement — through APIs and platforms used by businesses.

How do I integrate stablecoin payments into my product?

You can integrate stablecoin payments through an API platform that provides client accounts, on-ramp and off-ramp, payouts, and settlement, without building the full infrastructure stack yourself.

What is YouHodler Business API?

YouHodler Business API is digital asset infrastructure for businesses that manage funds flows inside their products. It enables client accounts, stablecoin payments, payouts, on-ramp, off-ramp, conversion, settlement, controls, and internal reporting inside your product.

Who is it built for?

Payment institutions, EMIs, cross-border businesses, fintechs, embedded finance platforms, treasury teams, and teams building automation workflows.

What flows can I launch?

Client accounts, funding, stablecoin payments, payouts, on-ramp, off-ramp, conversion, and settlement — all inside your product.

How fast can we start?

Start in sandbox, test your first flow in days, and move toward production after technical review — saving years of infrastructure build work.

Do we need to build our own infrastructure?

No. You use YouHodler's infrastructure for accounts, liquidity, internal records, and settlement, while keeping your own product and user experience.

Can we keep our own product and brand?

Yes. Your product stays in front. YouHodler powers the infrastructure behind the scenes.

How are controls and approvals handled?

Roles, limits, and approval records can support how funds movement is configured before execution.

Is this secure and audit-ready?

Operations are traceable through event history, account activity, and internal reporting, with structured controls and settlement visibility.

How does API access work?

You integrate via scoped service accounts with controlled permissions and event-driven updates through webhooks.

Can this be used for automation or AI-driven flows?

Yes. The platform is API-first and event-driven, making it suitable for automated workflows and AI-assisted systems.

What makes it ready for automation?

Clear execution rules, scoped access, and event-driven flows allow automation systems and AI-assisted workflows to operate safely within defined boundaries.
